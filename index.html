Understanding U.S. Financial Commitments to Ukraine

Two aid packages approved by Congress have brought the U.S.’s total financial commitments to Ukraine to $54 billion. But how is that money being used? And how does it compare to other nations that have pledged to support Ukraine?

By Hannah-Kathryn Valles
Oct. 18, 2022

In response to Russia's invasion of Ukraine on February 24, 2022, numerous world leaders pledged to assist Ukraine in its fight against Russian aggression. Since the start of the conflict, the United States has committed roughly $54 billion in support to Ukraine—an asymmetrical sum when compared to contributions made by European Union member nations.

According to the Khiel Institute for the World Economy, the U.S. is the largest bilateral supporter of Ukraine, making up 56% of total commitments from 40 donor nations and institutions. Here are the top four areas of aid spending:

Weapons

$12.5 billion allocated for weapons procurement, including missiles, combat vehicles, aircraft, ammunition, and defense-wide discretionary funding distributed to the United States Armed Forces. Resources for research, development, testing, and evaluation of weapons systems.

Economic Support

$9.8 billion made available for international disaster assistance. Includes funding for the provision of emergency food and shelter as well as programs to combat human trafficking. Desginated funds to assist vulnerable populations through local and international governmental organizations.

Military Personnel

$8.1 billion approved for the operation and maintenance of the U.S. armed forces. Provides intelligence support and finances the deployment of troops to allied countries. Sum includes hardship duty pay.

Food & Medical Assistance

$7 billion allocated for food assistance, health care, and other humanitarian aid. Program funding includes medical support, screening, and other public health activities for displaced Ukrainian populations, both domestically and internationally.
